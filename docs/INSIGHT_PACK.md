## Visual 1 — Distribution of Objective Mean Score (2020–2024)

**What was measured:** Density of (CN+CH+LC+MT)/4 per year; same scale 0–1000.

**What changed (2020–2024):** Shift in mean/median and shape of the distribution by year (e.g. post-2021 recovery or stability). Exact values come from the notebook summary (mean, median per year).

**Why it matters:** A sustained shift in the distribution changes cut-offs for selection and the share of candidates above any threshold; stability supports comparable year-on-year KPIs.

**Who should care:** Universities (cut-off calibration), Secretarias (benchmark), EdTechs (content targeting).

**Action suggestion:** If the distribution shifted up after 2021, recalibrate “good performance” thresholds and communication. If variance increased, target support where the left tail thickened (by UF or profile).

## Visual 2 — Redação Score Distribution (% ≥800)

**What was measured:** Redação score density by year and % of candidates with redação ≥800 per year.

**What changed (2020–2024):** Change in % ≥800 between 2020 and 2024 (e.g. +X p.p. or −X p.p.). Tail shape shows whether excellence became more or less concentrated.

**Why it matters:** Redação ≥800 drives top placements and scholarships; trend in this share affects programme design and equity (who reaches excellence).

**Who should care:** Universities (scholarship and cut-offs), Cursinhos and EdTechs (redação focus), Secretarias (writing policies).

**Action suggestion:** If % ≥800 grew, reinforce redação in UFs or profiles that did not improve. If it fell, prioritise writing and feedback in underperforming clusters.

## Visual 3 — Presence Rate by Year

**What was measured:** % of participants fully present (all four objective exams) by year; Gold reflects the valid cohort (100% present by construction).

**What changed (2020–2024):** In Gold this is 100% per year. To see real change in presence, the same metric must be computed on Silver (pre–valid-cohort filter). The visual documents the cohort definition.

**Why it matters:** Presence drives comparability of means and KPIs; a drop in raw presence in 2020–2021 would explain lower valid N and different demographics in the cohort.

**Who should care:** Secretarias (attendance campaigns), Analysts (interpretation of 2020–2021).

**Action suggestion:** Compute presence rate from Silver by year and profile; if 2020–2021 show lower presence, target attendance and communication in similar future crises and report “valid cohort” vs “all enrolled” in dashboards.

## Visual 4 — Average Score by UF (Top 10 Growth 2020 vs 2024)

**What was measured:** Mean objective score (media_objetiva) by UF in 2020 and 2024; delta = 2024 − 2020; top 10 UFs by delta.

**What changed (2020–2024):** UFs with largest positive delta (e.g. +Y points). Measurable list of “biggest improvers” and their 2020 vs 2024 levels.

**Why it matters:** Identifies where performance gains were strongest for targeting (replication) or where baseline was low and growth is a priority (support).

**Who should care:** Secretarias (where to scale what works), EdTechs and Cursinhos (geographic focus), Universities (regional recruitment).

**Action suggestion:** Prioritise math and objective-area reinforcement in UFs that still rank low despite growth. Replicate practices from top-growth UFs in neighbouring states.

## Visual 5 — Gap by Income (Q006: Lowest vs Highest) Over Time

**What was measured:** Gap = mean(media_objetiva) for highest income bracket (Q) minus mean for lowest (A), by year (2020–2024).

**What changed (2020–2024):** Increase or decrease in the gap (points) over the period. Widening = rising inequality in measured performance; narrowing = convergence.

**Why it matters:** Equity goals (quotas, scholarships, targeting) depend on whether the income–performance gap is stable, widening or narrowing.

**Who should care:** Secretarias and policy analysts (equity), Universities (scholarship and affirmative action), Cursinhos (free prep targeting).

**Action suggestion:** If the gap widened, target scholarships and prep to low-income clusters (e.g. Q006 A–C) and monitor gap in the next cycle. If it narrowed, document and scale the policies or programmes associated with that period.

## Visual 6 — Performance by Gender (Mean Objective Score) Over Years

**What was measured:** Mean media_objetiva by sex (M/F) and year (2020–2024); same scale 0–1000.

**What changed (2020–2024):** Difference in levels and trend between M and F (e.g. “F mean increased by X points; M by Y”; gap widened/narrowed by Z points).

**Why it matters:** Fairness in selection and targeting; trend informs whether gender-focused actions are associated with convergence or divergence.

**Who should care:** Universities (fairness and communication), Secretarias (gender equity), EdTechs (content and messaging).

**Action suggestion:** If one group lags or the gap grows, prioritise support (content, mentoring, scholarships) for the lagging group and avoid generic messaging that might widen the gap. Tie actions to the specific subject or area where the gap is largest (e.g. math vs languages).
